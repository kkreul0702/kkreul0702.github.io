I am currently a PhD Student

During my physics bachelor's degree, I was introduced to particle physics and was immediately drawn to the interplay of programming and data analysis with the complex physics in this field. In my bachelor project, I worked on improving the modelling of the decay of hadrons in the C++ program SHERPA. For my master's degree, I specialized in particle physics and completed my project on the unfolding of the di-photon 13 TeV ATLAS analysis. This work was an essential part of the publication and allows for an easy interpretation and comparison of the data with theoretical models. In my PhD project, I am the main analyser of the 13 TeV s-channel single top analysis, responsible for the full analysis chain from the data exploration to the multivariate analysis and the statistical modeling. The goal of this analysis is to discover the predicted but not yet found s-channel single top production, despite the exceptionally small signal contribution (below 5%).

I utilized a specialized analysis technique, the Matrix Element Method (MEM), to separate the signal from the background contribution. I constantly searched for ways to optimize this method. I updated the description of the detector and fine tuned parameters (More on that here). I also explored a new way of combining intermediate results of the MEM and kinematic variables with a neural network (Look here). While particle physics is an interesting field, I am now seeking to have more impact with my work. I am highly motivated to learn new skills and technologies, and I am confident in my ability to adapt quickly to new challenges. With my experience in handling large datasets and my natural curiosity, I am eager to apply my knowledge to real-world problems.

My PhD project

Neural Net Project
